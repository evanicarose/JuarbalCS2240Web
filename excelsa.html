Excelsa Coffee


Desirable with Unique Flavors

Excelsa coffee, discovered in Africa at the start of the 20th century, remains relatively obscure in the global coffee market. While its exact trade and consumption figures are unclear due to its minimal presence, it has been cultivated and consumed in Africa and Asia for years. Despite this, its origin, often misunderstood, has led to improper cultivation and processing, resulting in a reputation for low cup quality and lack of distinctive flavor.

Much like robusta, excelsa has the potential to yield beans with desirable and unique flavors when farmed and processed carefully. Originally discovered in Central Africa in 1903 and later reclassified as a variety of the liberica species, excelsa faces confusion in labeling and classification, contributing to unreliable production data and decreased quality. However, its lack of awareness, coupled with the absence of a commodity market and standardized pricing, presents significant obstacles to its widespread adoption. Nonetheless, continued research, experimentation, and buyer feedback may help establish excelsa as another specialty option for consumers, offering a glimpse into the untapped potential of this resilient coffee species.
